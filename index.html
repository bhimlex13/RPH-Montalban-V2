Introduction

The Municipality of Rodriguez, formerly and still popularly known as Montalban, is a first-class municipality in the Philippine province of Rizal. The local place of Montalban was founded by Padre Eustaquio, a native of Pasig, as a town in early 1871 during the Spanish government. The Balite trees that boldly stand on the banks of the Montalban River gave rise to the name "Balite" for the barrio, as given by the locals. Later on, as the town was surrounded by mountains, the Spanish changed the name to "Montalban," derived from the word "Monte," which means mountain. Lastly, the place of the Municipality of Montalban lies quietly at the foot of closely ranged mountains, occupying approximately 27,290 hectares of land.

The History of Montalban

Montalban, a town in the Philippines, has a captivating history that encompasses ancient civilizations, colonial struggles, and modern transformations. The town's roots can be traced back to indigenous communities that thrived in harmony with nature, cultivating crops, and engaging in trade. Spanish colonization in the 16th century left an enduring imprint on Montalban, shaping its culture, architecture, and religion. The town played a significant role in the fight for Philippine independence during the 19th century, with local heroes like Gat Andres Bonifacio inspiring and mobilizing the masses.


Montalban also endured the hardships of Japanese occupation during World War II, showcasing the resilience and bravery of its people. In the post-war era, Montalban experienced rapid urbanization and became a

The Historical And Heritage Sites of Montalban

The historical and heritage sites of Montalban, now known as Rodriguez, Rizal, boast several notable landmarks. Some of these include Wawa Dam, a pre-World War II structure; Pamitinan Cave, where the Katipunan was founded; and Mount Pamitinan, a site of great significance in Philippine history.
